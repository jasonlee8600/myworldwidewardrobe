My World Wide Wardrobe

H&M, B. Cools, Vans

Here, I decided to try on an outfit comprised mainly of my favorite color: orange. Brown is my favorite color to wear, but orange has always been my favorite color in general. Although not a true orange, this peachy shade is still very appealing to me.

This image was taken against a brick wall in Silliman. The white tile floor complimented the color, position, and pattern of my shoes perfectly. I posed with my head down and hands in my pockets, but I got a little spicy with the foot up against the wall. This picture may look nice, but it was really awkward to ask a random student leaving the dining hall to take it for me.

I thrifted this shirt at my local Burlington Coat Factory store, and the "B. Cools" on it is short for Barney Cools, a popular clothing company based in Australia. The gold necklaces from H&M also go well with the yellow wording on the shirt as the secondary color for this outfit. The peach shorts are from a Vans outlet store, and they are my favorite shorts to wear during the summertime.

The sneakers I am wearing are the Vans Checkerboard Slip-ons. Vans shoes were originally popular within the skateboarding fashion community, but they've become sought after in streetwear because of their affordability. Mine are a little beat up because I wear these sneakers pretty often, but I like to think that this visible wear and tear gives the shoe some character. Lastly, the black, beaded bracelet was just a little accessory I threw on to add a little more black to the outfit.

This outfit deserves a checkered 4 out of 5 stars. I am a little biased because orange is my favorite color, but the contrast between the abstract shirt pattern and checkered shoe pattern really helps elevate this outfit.
